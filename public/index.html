Results for "how much nox is reduced by adblue":

Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF), also known as AdBlue, is a crucial component in Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems used in modern diesel vehicles. It is designed to significantly reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions, which are harmful pollutants. When injected into the exhaust stream, DEF undergoes a chemical reaction that converts NOx into harmless nitrogen gas and water vapor.DEF can reduce NOx emissions by **85% to 95%**, making it highly effective in helping vehicles meet stringent emission standards.

What is DEF?

DEF stands for Diesel Exhaust Fluid, a non-toxic, colorless, odorless, and non-flammable fluid that's injected into the exhaust system of diesel vehicles to reduce harmful emissions. It's also known as AdBlue.

Why DEF is Important?

Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) is a key component in diesel engines because it helps reduce harmful emissions and ensure that the vehicle meets emission standards. It achieves a remarkable 85 - 95% reduction in NOx emissions.
